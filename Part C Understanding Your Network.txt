Part C: Understanding Your Network

1. Which IP range does your device belong to?
192.168.0.0 to 192.168.255.255

2. Is it Public or Private?
Private IP range.

3. What role does your router play in your network?
The Traffic Director: When you send data to another computer on the same local network, the router (using its built-in switch) keeps that traffic local. When you request data from the outside internet, the router knows to send that request out through the modem.

Network Address Translation (NAT): Because your computer has a Private IP address, it cannot talk directly to the public internet. Your router acts as a middleman, taking your computer's private request, swapping the return address to its own Public IP, sending it to the internet, and then routing the response back to your specific device.

4. What would happen if the DNS server stopped working?
DNS server goes down, your internet connection is technically still active, but you cannot browse the web normally
